Core Boxing Techniques

Jab (Mat Trong)

The foundation of Muay Thai boxing. A quick, straight punch with the lead hand that sets up combinations, maintains distance, and disrupts your opponent's rhythm. Keep your shoulder high and snap the punch back quickly.

Cross (Mat Sai)

Your power punch. Thrown with the rear hand, rotating from the hips and driving through the target. The cross in Muay Thai often sets up devastating elbows and knees in the clinch.

Hook (Mat Wiang San)

A circular punch targeting the side of the head or body. In Muay Thai, hooks are particularly effective when transitioning from clinch work or after defending kicks with your arms.

Uppercut (Mat Soke)

An upward punch that targets the chin or body. Essential for close-range combat and clinch entries. Thai boxers use uppercuts to create openings for knee strikes.

Overhand (Mat Kratob)

A looping punch thrown over the opponent's guard. Particularly effective against southpaws and when your opponent has their hands low from throwing kicks.

Body Shots

Punches to the ribs, solar plexus, and liver. Body boxing in Muay Thai weakens opponents for later rounds and creates opportunities for head hunting.

Boxing Fundamentals

Muay Thai Stance

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lead foot slightly forward. Keep your weight evenly distributed, ready to check kicks or throw punches. Hands up, elbows tucked, chin down.

Guard Position

High guard with gloves protecting your temples and jaw. Unlike Western boxing, keep elbows close to defend against body kicks and knee strikes. Peek through your gloves.

Footwork

Small, controlled steps maintaining balance. Circle away from power hands, step in behind your jab, and always be ready to lift your knee to check incoming kicks.

Head Movement

Subtle slips and weaves. Avoid excessive bobbing that leaves you vulnerable to knees. Small movements with quick returns to guard position.

Range Management

Control the distance with jabs and footwork. Stay at punching range when advantageous, but be ready to clinch or move to kicking range when needed.

Defense Integration

Boxing defense must account for kicks, knees, and elbows. Keep one eye on incoming punches and another on potential leg kicks or clinch attempts.

Essential Combinations

  • Jab-Cross
    The fundamental 1-2 combo. Sets up kicks, elbows, or clinch entries. Follow with a low kick or knee.
  • Jab-Cross-Hook
    Classic three-punch combination. The hook can target head or body, creating openings for knee strikes.
  • Double Jab-Cross
    Use the double jab to close distance and land a hard cross. Perfect setup for clinch work.
  • Jab-Uppercut-Hook
    Work the head and body levels. The uppercut lifts the guard for the hook to land clean.
  • Cross-Hook-Uppercut
    Power combination starting with the rear hand. Each punch opens up the next target.
  • Body-Head Combinations
    Attack the body to bring the guard down, then target the head. Essential for breaking tough defenses.

Training Methods

Heavy Bag Work

Focus on power and technique. Mix punching combinations with kicks and knees. Practice boxing while in orthodox and southpaw stances to develop ambidextrous skills.

Pad Training

Work with a partner holding Thai pads. Practice combinations that flow from punches to kicks to clinch work. Develop timing and accuracy under pressure.

Shadow Boxing

Visualize opponents and practice combinations. Include defensive movements and transitions between boxing, kicking, and clinch ranges. Focus on form and flow.

Sparring Integration

Practice boxing within the full Muay Thai context. Learn to punch while defending kicks, transition from boxing to clinch, and use punches to set up other techniques.
